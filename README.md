# Professional Ethics & Responsible Conduct Statement

> *"Technology is a mechanism for protection, not exploitation. My work in cybersecurity is guided by a clear duty to defend systems, safeguard data, and act with uncompromising integrity in every environment I operate."*

---

### Core Ethical Principles

* **1. Strict Scope & Authorization**  
  I operate strictly within authorized parameters, explicit permissions, and legal frameworks. I do not engage in unauthorized testing, credential harvesting, or intrusive analysis outside designated, fully sanctioned lab or enterprise environments.

* **2. Data Privacy & Confidentiality**  
  I view organizational and user data as a fundamental trust asset. Maintaining confidentiality, system integrity, and privacy rights is a non-negotiable standard across all SIEM monitoring, threat detection, and incident response activities.

* **3. Responsible Disclosure**  
  When identifying security gaps, misconfigurations, or software vulnerabilities, I follow established responsible disclosure protocols—prioritizing system remediation and defensive resilience over exposure.

* **4. Discipline & Accountability**  
  True mastery requires continuous self-auditing and accountability. I take direct ownership of my technical actions, log analysis, and alert configurations, ensuring complete transparency and honesty in all security reporting.
